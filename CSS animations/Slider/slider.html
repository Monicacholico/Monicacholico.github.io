The Road to a Vaccinated World
Countries across the globe are joining forces to protect and vaccinate as many people as possible against Covid-19. Here, some of the most influential voices leading the charge speak about what it will take to vaccinate the world's most vulnerable populations.
“We must demonstrate a unity of purpose and work collectively to wage and win the war against Covid-19. We use a simple equation at Africa CDC: vaccines + vaccinations = lives saved. Moving forward, we should have many countries manufacturing diagnostics and vaccines, and engage in local manufacturing of vaccines. Africa, and the world in general, will approach future public health issues with more scrutiny and greater consideration to equitable access.”
––
Dr. John Nkengasong, Director, Africa Centers for Disease Control and Prevention
“The seeds of a two-class world have already been sown: those living in vaccinated nations recovering and returning to the lifestyles they missed, while the unvaccinated are facing new waves of Covid, with all its death and illness and devastating economic fallout. Global vaccine rollout is the only way to end Covid-19. There is no more urgent investment. Now we need to look beyond borders and protect ourselves from Covid-19 as one world.”
––
Melissa Fleming, Under Secretary General for Global Communications, United Nations
“As we look toward the future, global leaders must find the courage to act collectively so that the world can emerge from this pandemic stronger, more united and better prepared for future epidemic and pandemic threats. CEPI aims to support the development of vaccines that are effective against SARS-CoV-2 variants and other coronaviruses, including SARS and MERS. We also plan to develop prototype vaccines against many other classes of threat and are working with partners to compress the vaccine development process to 100 days.”
––
Richard Hatchett, C.E.O., Coalition for Epidemic Preparedness Innovations
“No one is safe from Covid-19 until we are all safe — this is why international collaboration is the only way out of this pandemic. The U.K. is proud to be one of the earliest and largest donors to Covax [Covid-19 Vaccines Global Access] to support global access to Covid-19 vaccines for the world’s most vulnerable populations. Through Covax, millions of safe and effective vaccine doses have been delivered to over 120 countries and territories.”
––
Wendy Morton, Minister for European Neighborhood and the Americas, U.K. Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office
