Dear Jonathan Ellis,

I believe that when information is presented the right way — visually, interactively, in a form that stays with you — it changes how people understand the world. That belief is the thread that connects everything I've built over the past fifteen years, and it's why I'm applying for the Data Graphics/Multimedia Editor position on The Upshot.

In 2011, while pursuing a master's in gender and social policy, I founded Planeta Mujer — a digital platform born from a simple conviction: people make better decisions when they have access to clear, honest information. The original site translated complex gender policy research into accessible content and online courses for a Spanish-speaking audience. Last year, I rebuilt it as an interactive data visualization platform. "50 Million," a scroll-driven narrative on modern slavery, uses 10,000 HTML Canvas particles that animate into country shapes through D3 force simulation, contextualizing 50 million victims against populations and historical atrocities. I also built an interactive scrollytelling world map of World Bank gender equality scores across 190 countries, an SVG hourglass visualization comparing gendered time use across 30 OECD countries, and an interactive sunburst taxonomy of feminist thought. Every piece begins with the same question: how do I make this information impossible to look away from?

That instinct started even before I knew how to code. In 2014, I saw a business opportunity importing ballet pointe shoes to Mexico and needed to build an e-commerce site — so I taught myself web development. To rank in search, I started writing articles about ballet. What began as an SEO strategy became something I loved: I've now published over 140 bilingual explanatory articles on technique, anatomy, history, and culture. I discovered that the satisfaction of explaining something clearly — making the unfamiliar feel familiar — is what drives me most.

For the past four years as a front-end engineer at The New York Times, I've been building interactive, content-driven applications on the advertising side. I collaborate daily with designers, editorial teams, and product developers. I've seen from the inside how this organization works — its commitment to quality, its pace, its culture of accuracy. But I've always been drawn to the journalism itself. The Upshot's mission — to help readers understand the news through creative, data-driven explanation — is exactly the kind of work I've been building toward on my own time with Planeta Mujer.

What I'd bring to The Upshot is a combination that's hard to find in one person: the engineering skills to build interactive visualizations from scratch (D3.js, Canvas, Scrollama, Python, R), years of published explanatory writing, a master's-level grounding in gender and social policy, and the investigative instincts I developed as a fraud investigator at the U.S. Embassy in Mexico City — where building a case meant finding patterns in data and making complex systems legible. I'm also bilingual, fluent in both English and Spanish, and currently completing NYU's Modern Journalism certificate to sharpen my reporting and multimedia storytelling skills. I'm deeply interested in the topics The Upshot covers: policy, gender, immigration, economics, and education.

I would love the opportunity to bring my curiosity, my technical skills, and my passion for making information matter to The Upshot's team.

Sincerely,
Monica Cholico
